ANTHONY BURGESS

Books

Dystopia / Science Fiction / Social Critique

  1. A Clockwork Orange – 1962 His most famous work; a novel set in a totalitarian society, dealing with violence and free will.
  2. The Wanting Seed – 1962 Contains themes of overpopulation, social policy, and cyclical history.
  3. 1985 – 1978 A response to George Orwell’s 1984; focusing on unions and cultural decline.

Historical Novels

  1. Nothing Like the Sun – 1964 A fictionalized account of William Shakespeare’s life.
  2. Napoleon Symphony – 1974 A novel about Napoleon’s life, structured around Beethoven’s 3rd Symphony (Eroica).

The Indonesia Trilogy (The Malayan Trilogy / The Empire Trilogy)

Satirical novels set in Southeast Asia during the British colonial period

  1. Time for a Tiger – 1956
  2. The Enemy in the Blanket – 1958
  3. Beds in the East – 1959

Selected and Experimental Novels

  1. Earthly Powers – 1980 An epic narrative; a major novel about the relationship between a writer and religion. One of his most comprehensive and acclaimed works.
  2. The End of the World News – 1982 A postmodern novel blending three different stories (biography, musical, dystopia).
  3. Beard’s Roman Women – 1976 A novel about grief, love, and art.
  4. The Pianoplayers – 1986 Focuses on characters with a musical background. Contains autobiographical elements about his father.
